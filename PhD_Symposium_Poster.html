Are Genera Evolutionarily Real?
Evidence from the Phylogenetic Reconstruction of Solanaceae
University of Edinburgh · SBS PGR Symposium
🎯 Research Question

The genus is a fundamental unit in biological classification, yet its evolutionary reality remains uncertain.

Are genera:

  • Monophyletic units with evolutionary meaning?
  • Arbitrary classifications of continuous variation?
This study tests whether genera represent true evolutionary lineages using phylogenetic methods.
🔬 Methodology

Supermatrix Phylogenetic Approach:

Molecular Data
9 nuclear and plastid loci
Taxon Sampling
1,581 → 2,000 species
Data Curation
GenBank sequences curated and aligned
Analysis Method
GMYC model assessment
🌿 Study System

Solanaceae (Nightshade Family):

  • ~2,000–3,000 described species
  • ~50–100 recognized genera
  • Economically and medically important
  • Phylogenetically diverse
  • Ideal for testing genus delimitation
📊 Expected Outcomes
  • Determine if current genera are monophyletic clades
  • Identify genera requiring phylogenetic revision
  • Provide evidence-based genus delimitation framework
  • Improve Solanaceae taxonomy and classification
Goal: Support monophyletic genera and inform phylogenetically focused delimitations
